ASSUMPTIONS

  • Assume that both of the semi-infinite regions are either vaccuum or perfect/ideal dielectric (i.e. conductivity, σ = 0)
  • We will assume that the coordinate axes are aligned along the interface and the wave is assumed to travel at an angle wrt media interface
  • If depicted on paper the x-z plane will lie on the paper whereas the y-axis will be along the normal to the plane of paper
  • Assume the plane of interface to be along x-y plane and normal to the plane is z-axis. Plane x-y cuts z-axis at z=0.
  • Assume that the media are isotropic in their respective semi-infinite region
  • Value of all angles and phase are in radians
  • Assume that polarisation is either parallel or perpendicular
Every plane wave in an arbitrary direction can be written as F = Fₒexp(-j.K.β)
This equation can be generalised as:
E = [(a+jb)xˆ+ (c+jd)yˆ+ (r+js)zˆ]exp[-j.β.(Xcos(φx)+Ycos(φy)+Zcos(φz))]

where,
j = complex imaginary quantity analogous to i
a,c,r = real coefficients
b,d,s = complex coefficients
xˆ,yˆ,zˆ = unit vectors in x, y, z axes respectively
Eₒ = [(a+jb)xˆ+ (c+jd)yˆ+ (r+js)zˆ]
β = phase constant of the wave
k = (Xcos(φx)+Ycos(φy)+Zcos(φz)), the dot product of normal vector and displacement vector from the origin
φx = Angle made by the incident wave wrt X-Axis
φy = Angle made by the incident wave wrt Y-Axis
φz = Angle made by the incident wave wrt Z-Axis
